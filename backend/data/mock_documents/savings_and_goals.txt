CREATING A SAVINGS GOAL

Step-by-Step Instructions:
1. Navigate to the "Goals" tab in the bottom navigation bar
2. Tap the "Create New Goal" button (plus icon)
3. Enter goal details:
   - Goal Name: Choose a meaningful name (e.g., "Emergency Fund", "Dream Vacation")
   - Current Amount: How much you've already saved toward this goal
   - Target Amount: Your final goal amount
   - Deadline: Target date to achieve goal (optional but recommended)
   - Category: Select icon and color theme for personalization
4. Review and confirm your goal settings
5. Click "Create Goal" to save

Tips for Effective Goal Setting:
- Be specific with amounts (not "save money" but "save $10,000")
- Set realistic deadlines based on your income
- Break large goals into smaller milestones
- Start with 1-3 goals to avoid overwhelm
- Review and adjust goals quarterly

MONEY MANAGEMENT STRATEGIES

The 50/30/20 Budgeting Rule:
One of the most popular and effective budgeting frameworks:

50% - Needs (Essential Expenses):
- Housing (rent/mortgage)
- Utilities (electric, water, gas, internet)
- Groceries and food
- Transportation (car payment, gas, insurance, public transit)
- Insurance (health, life, disability)
- Minimum debt payments
- Childcare
- Essential clothing

30% - Wants (Discretionary Spending):
- Dining out and entertainment
- Hobbies and recreation
- Streaming services and subscriptions
- Vacations and travel
- Shopping (non-essential)
- Gym memberships
- Gifts
- Personal care beyond basics

20% - Savings and Debt Repayment:
- Emergency fund
- Retirement contributions
- Savings goals
- Extra debt payments (beyond minimum)
- Investments
- Education savings

Example for $5,000 Monthly Income:
- Needs: $2,500 (50%)
- Wants: $1,500 (30%)
- Savings: $1,000 (20%)

Adjustments:
- High cost-of-living areas: 60/20/20 or 55/25/20
- Aggressive savers: 50/20/30 or 50/10/40
- Debt payoff focus: 50/15/35

AUTO-SAVE FEATURES

How Auto-Save Works:
- Automatically transfers money to savings
- Set your schedule (daily, weekly, biweekly, monthly)
- Choose amount or percentage of income
- Select source account (checking)
- Select destination (savings goal or savings account)
- Cancel anytime without penalty

Setup Instructions:
1. Go to Profile > Auto-Save Settings
2. Toggle Auto-Save ON
3. Set frequency:
   - Daily: Small amounts ($5-$10)
   - Weekly: Moderate amounts ($50-$100)
   - Biweekly: Aligned with paychecks ($200-$500)
   - Monthly: Larger transfers ($500-$2,000+)
4. Set amount or percentage:
   - Fixed: Same amount each time
   - Percentage: % of checking balance
   - Income %: % of direct deposits
5. Choose destination goal
6. Confirm and activate

Auto-Save Strategies:
- "Pay yourself first": Transfer on payday
- Round-up savings: See below
- Weekly micro-saves: $10/week = $520/year
- Bonus savings: Auto-save windfalls

ROUND-UP SAVINGS

How Round-Up Works:
- Rounds purchases to nearest dollar
- Saves the difference automatically
- Example: $4.50 coffee â†’ rounds to $5.00 â†’ saves $0.50
- Painless way to save
- Can accelerate significantly over time

Example Impact:
- 20 transactions/week with avg $0.50 round-up = $10/week = $520/year
- 50 transactions/week = $25/week = $1,300/year

Setup:
1. Profile > Auto-Save > Round-Up Savings
2. Toggle ON
3. Select multiplier:
   - 1x: Round to nearest dollar
   - 2x: Double the round-up amount
   - 5x: Five times the round-up
   - 10x: Aggressive saving
4. Choose destination goal
5. Review and activate

Tips:
- Start with 1x multiplier
- Increase as you adjust
- Monitor to ensure not overdrawing
- Pairs well with regular Auto-Save

INVESTMENT AND WEALTH BUILDING

Beyond Savings - Growing Wealth:

Why Invest?
- Savings accounts: 0.05% - 4.25% returns
- Stock market: 7-10% average annual returns
- Real estate: 8-12% average annual returns
- Beat inflation (typically 2-3% annually)
- Build long-term wealth faster

Investment Options:

Index Funds:
- Diversified stocks in single fund
- Tracks market index (S&P 500)
- Low fees (0.03% - 0.20%)
- Automatic diversification
- Recommended: 60-80% of portfolio
- Risk: Moderate
- Timeline: 5+ years

Target-Date Funds:
- Automatically adjusts risk as you age
- Set target retirement year
- Becomes more conservative over time
- Good for hands-off investors
- Available in 401(k) and IRAs

Individual Stocks:
- Higher risk, higher potential return
- Requires research and monitoring
- Recommended: Only 5-10% of portfolio
- Best for: Experienced investors
- Diversify across sectors

Bonds:
- Lower risk than stocks
- Steady interest income
- Government bonds: Safest
- Corporate bonds: Higher yield, more risk
- Recommended: 20-40% of portfolio
- Increases with age

Real Estate Investment Trusts (REITs):
- Invest in real estate without buying property
- Regular dividend income
- Traded like stocks
- Diversification benefit
- Recommended: 5-15% of portfolio

Retirement Account Options:

401(k):
- Employer-sponsored
- 2025 contribution limit: $23,000/year
- Employer match: FREE MONEY (contribute enough to get full match!)
- Tax-deferred growth
- Withdrawals taxed in retirement
- Early withdrawal penalty: 10% before age 59Â½

Traditional IRA:
- Individual retirement account
- 2025 contribution limit: $7,000/year ($8,000 if 50+)
- Tax-deductible contributions
- Tax-deferred growth
- Withdrawals taxed in retirement
- More investment options than 401(k)

Roth IRA:
- Contribute after-tax dollars
- Tax-FREE growth and withdrawals
- Income limits apply
- Contributions can be withdrawn anytime
- Best for: Young investors, those expecting higher future tax rates
- 2025 limit: $7,000/year ($8,000 if 50+)
